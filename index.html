How It Works

Four simple steps that give you the intelligence you need to keep your best people.

1

Monthly Crew Interviews (English & Spanish)

We call each crew member in their preferred language and ask the same questions every month: "What's going well? What's frustrating? Any concerns? How's morale?"

Our bilingual team conducts interviews in English or Spanish, ensuring every crew member can share feedback comfortably and honestly.

Because WE'RE asking (not you), and in THEIR language, they tell us the truth.

Common insights we uncover:

RETENTION RISKS:

  • "I got an offer for $3 more per hour from another company"
  • "The commute is killing me - 90 minutes each way"
  • "I might move back to Mexico to be closer to family"

OPERATIONAL ISSUES:

  • "The mower keeps breaking down and we look unprofessional"
  • "We're always running out of materials on job sites"
  • "The new guy doesn't know what he's doing and it's slowing us down"

MORALE PROBLEMS:

  • "Nobody says thank you when we finish a hard job"
  • "The schedule changes constantly and I can't plan anything"
  • "El supervisor no escucha cuando le decimos que hay un problema"
2

Pattern Analysis Across Your Entire Team

We compare responses across your entire team to spot trends you can't see when talking to people one-on-one.

What we discover:
  • 3 people mentioned equipment issues = operational blind spot you need to address
  • 2 people mentioned pay concerns = retention risk brewing
  • Everyone praised the new lead tech = morale boost worth recognizing
  • Spanish-speaking crew mentioned communication gaps = cultural issue to fix

You get the big picture, not just individual complaints.

3

One-Page Executive Report

You receive a simple summary showing:

  • Overall morale score (High/Medium/Low)
  • Top 3 things going well
  • Top 3 concerns or frustrations
  • Red flags (who's at risk of quitting)
  • Suggested focus areas

No fluff. Just what you need to know. Plus detailed notes from each interview organized in a spreadsheet for your records.

4

You Take Action (While There's Still Time)

Armed with real intelligence, you can:

  • Address the equipment issue before more people get frustrated
  • Have a retention conversation with the at-risk employee
  • Give recognition for what's actually working
  • Fix cultural or communication problems early
  • Stop problems BEFORE people quit
Most owners discover 2-3 issues they had no idea about in the first month alone.
